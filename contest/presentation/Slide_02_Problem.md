# Slide 2: The Problem

## Content

**Incident Response is Broken**

- **Slow MTTR:** 4+ hours for common incidents
- **Repetitive Toil:** 70% of incidents follow known patterns
- **Knowledge Silos:** Runbooks lost in wikis, tribal knowledge
- **On-Call Burnout:** 50+ alerts per night, most noise
- **Inconsistent Response:** Every engineer handles incidents differently

**Key Statistic:** Most enterprises spend 70% of incident time on diagnosis, not resolution.

## Design Notes

- Five bullet points with icon each (clock, gear, book, person, chart)
- Problem statement in red/amber accent
- Statistics highlighted in bold

## Speaker Notes

"Incident response hasn't evolved with infrastructure complexity. Engineers spend most of their time diagnosing problems that have been solved before — but the knowledge is scattered across wikis, chat threads, and people's heads."
