The Problem: Why We Built This
==============================

Keeping computer systems running smoothly is really hard. Here's why:

1. Fixing Problems Takes Way Too Long
-------------------------------------
When something breaks, engineers have to figure out what's wrong first. This takes:
- 15-30 minutes just to figure out how serious it is
- 1-4 hours to find the root cause for tricky problems
- 30-60 minutes for each fix action
- 15-30 minutes to check if the fix worked
- Total: 4-8 hours for a single incident (industry average)

2. Engineers Are Burned Out
---------------------------
- Someone has to be on-call 24/7 to handle problems
- About 60% of alerts are simple issues that follow known procedures
- Engineers spend 30% of their on-call time doing things a computer could do
- Too many alerts means real emergencies get missed

3. Knowledge Gets Lost
----------------------
- When experienced engineers leave, their knowledge leaves with them
- Runbooks (fix instructions) are rarely updated
- Different engineers fix the same problem in different ways
- No way to learn from past incidents

4. Fixes Are Inconsistent
-------------------------
- Different engineers diagnose the same symptoms differently
- Quality of fixes varies from person to person
- Validation steps are often skipped
- Documentation is incomplete

5. Problems Grow With Scale
---------------------------
- As companies grow, the number of incidents grows too
- Hiring more people helps with volume but not with complexity
- Senior engineers spend more time fighting fires than improving systems

What A Good Solution Should Do
------------------------------
- Automatically bring in incidents from monitoring tools
- Understand what's affected and how bad it is
- Search past incidents and runbooks for similar situations
- Create a clear, step-by-step fix plan
- Execute fixes safely with human approval for risky actions
- Monitor during and after the fix
- Validate that everything is working again
- Escalate to humans when needed
- Learn from outcomes to get better over time

Our platform does ALL of these except learning from outcomes (that's next on the roadmap).
