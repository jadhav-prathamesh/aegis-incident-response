Real Problems Our Customers Face (And How We Solve Them)
==========================================================

Who Needs This?
---------------
Large IT operations teams managing 500+ servers and complex systems. Specifically:
- Head of Site Reliability Engineering — responsible for keeping systems up and running
- On-call production engineers — the people who get woken up at 3 AM

Problem 1: Too Many Alerts
--------------------------
The Problem: Engineers get hundreds of alerts per day. Most are noise. Real emergencies get buried in the noise.

How We Solve It: Our Observer Agent filters and correlates alerts. It only flags what actually matters. The system groups related alerts together so engineers see the big picture, not individual noise.

Problem 2: Finding the Root Cause Takes Forever
------------------------------------------------
The Problem: When something breaks, engineers manually check logs, metrics, recent changes, and past incidents to find the cause. This takes 1-4 hours.

How We Solve It: The Planner Agent automatically searches everything:
- Past similar incidents (vector similarity search)
- Knowledge base runbooks
- Incident context (severity, category, affected services)
All of this happens in seconds, not hours.

Problem 3: Manual Fixes Are Slow and Error-Prone
-------------------------------------------------
The Problem: Common fixes (restart, scale up, clear cache) require engineers to manually SSH into servers or run kubectl commands. Each manual step risks human error.

How We Solve It: The Executor Agent automates 15 types of fix actions. Everything is logged, repeatable, and has built-in safety checks. No more "I accidentally ran the command on the wrong server."

Problem 4: Approval Bottlenecks
-------------------------------
The Problem: Actions like restarting a production database require manager approval, which causes delays while waiting for someone to respond.

How We Solve It: Our approval workflow handles request/approve/reject lifecycle. 8 types of risky actions always require approval. In development mode, actions auto-approve for faster iteration.

Problem 5: No One Checks If the Fix Actually Worked
---------------------------------------------------
The Problem: After applying a fix, teams often skip validation or just do a quick glance. The fix might not have worked, or might have caused a different problem.

How We Solve It: The Validator Agent runs 6+ types of checks automatically:
- Health endpoint checks
- Key metrics comparison
- Synthetic transactions
- Alert resolution verification
- Compliance checks
- Baseline comparison

Problem 6: Knowledge Walks Out the Door
----------------------------------------
The Problem: When experienced engineers leave, their incident knowledge leaves with them. The next person has to figure everything out from scratch.

How We Solve It: Every incident is automatically indexed for future search. Every resolution becomes searchable knowledge. Our hybrid search finds relevant past incidents by meaning, not just by keywords.

What We DON'T Solve Yet
-----------------------
- Predicting failures before they happen (configured but not built)
- Multi-cloud operations (AWS, GCP, Azure — just stubs)
- Integration with ticketing systems like ServiceNow (just stubs)
- Cost optimization (disabled by default)
