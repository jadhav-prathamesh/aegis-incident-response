Business Value: What Does This Save?
======================================

Important Note: All numbers below are estimates based on industry research. We haven't deployed this to a real customer yet.

What We Can Measure From The Code
---------------------------------
- 15 types of automated fix actions
- 7 incident lifecycle states
- 6 types of validation checks
- 4 types of monitoring checks
- 2 vector database backends
- 23 API endpoints
- 93 passing tests

Estimated Savings (For a Typical Large Company)
------------------------------------------------
Let's say a company has 500+ services and 50 engineers on rotation:

- Current average time to fix an incident: 4 hours
- With our platform: 30-60 minutes for common incidents
- Savings: 75-87% time reduction

- Current engineering hours per month spent on incidents: 400 hours
- With our platform: ~120 hours
- Savings: 280 hours per month

- Current monthly cost (at $80/hour engineer cost): $32,000
- With our platform: $9,600
- Annual savings: Approximately $269,000

Non-Financial Benefits (Equally Important)
------------------------------------------
1. Less burnout — engineers don't get woken up for routine issues
2. Consistent response — every incident handled the same way, every time
3. Automatic documentation — every action is logged, no one has to write post-mortems
4. Faster troubleshooting — similar-incident search shows past solutions instantly
5. Knowledge retention — when people leave, their knowledge stays in the system

How We're Different From Existing Tools
---------------------------------------
- Our multi-agent AI coordinates end-to-end, not just chatbots or simple rules
- Built-in approval workflow with auto-approve in development
- Hybrid knowledge search (meaning-based + keyword-based)
- Comprehensive validation (6 check types, not just health checks)
